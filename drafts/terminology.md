=========================================================
CAIA Terminology (Working Glossary)
Non-Normative Draft
=========================================================

STATUS
------
This document is a working glossary intended to improve precision and
shared understanding when discussing CAIA-based systems.

Definitions here are explanatory and may overlap with, but do not
override, normative definitions in the CAIA specification.

---------------------------------------------------------
1. PURPOSE
---------------------------------------------------------

The purpose of this glossary is to:
- Reduce semantic drift
- Improve cross-implementation communication
- Clarify overloaded terms

---------------------------------------------------------
2. TERM GUIDELINES
---------------------------------------------------------

Terms in this glossary:
- Are descriptive, not normative
- May evolve over time
- May include notes and caveats

---------------------------------------------------------
3. INITIAL TERMS (PLACEHOLDERS)
---------------------------------------------------------

Module
  A logical functional unit as defined by CAIA, independent of physical
  realization.

Coordinator
  A logical role responsible for orchestration or configuration within
  a CAIA system.

Interoperability
  The ability of independently implemented CAIA systems to interact
  predictably within the bounds of the specification.

(Additional terms to be added as needed.)

---------------------------------------------------------
4. RELATIONSHIP TO THE SPECIFICATION
---------------------------------------------------------

Where conflicts arise between this glossary and the CAIA specification,
the specification always takes precedence.
